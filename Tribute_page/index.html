Wangari Maathai was a Kenyan social, environmental and political activist. She was the first woman in Kenya to win a doctorate degree. She became the first African woman to win the Nobel Peace Prize a testament to her extraordinary courage, leadership, and vision. She is internationally recognized for her persistent struggle for democracy, human rights and environment conservation. She was the founder of the Green Belt movement. She authored four books: The Green Belt Movement; Unbowed: A Memoir; The Challenge for Africa; Replenishing the Earth
